## The Problem

### Performance management is broken — and costly.

Modern HR teams face a perfect storm of inefficiency:

**Manual review processes that consume hundreds of hours.** The average organization spends 210 hours per manager per year on performance review preparation, calibration, and documentation. Most of that time is wasted on administrative work, not actual development conversations.

**OKRs trapped in spreadsheets.** 73% of companies using OKRs still track them in Excel or Google Sheets. Without real-time visibility, goals go stale, progress is invisible to leadership, and alignment breaks down between teams and strategy.

**Unconscious bias corrupting review quality.** Research from MIT and Harvard shows that performance reviews contain measurable bias in 68% of cases — favoring certain demographics, penalizing personality traits rather than performance, and anchoring on recent events rather than full-period contributions. This costs companies retention, morale, and legal risk.

**No data-driven coaching.** Managers receive little guidance on how to develop their people. Coaching conversations are ad hoc, inconsistent, and not grounded in actual performance data. The result: high performers leave because they don't feel invested in; low performers stay because no one addresses the real issues.

**Siloed feedback that nobody acts on.** Most companies collect feedback in annual surveys that produce a PDF nobody reads. Real-time, actionable feedback loops simply don't exist for most teams.

## Key Features

### 1. Continuous OKR Tracking
Set objectives and key results at the individual, team, or department level. Track progress in real time with visual dashboards showing on-track, at-risk, and completed OKRs. Automated weekly digests notify managers of slipping goals before they become misses. Full update history shows exactly how progress evolved across the quarter, giving leadership the longitudinal data to improve goal-setting accuracy each cycle.

### 2. AI-Powered Bias Detection in Reviews
Every performance review submitted through TalentPulse is automatically analyzed by GPT-4o for 12 common bias patterns including: gender-coded language, recency bias, halo/horn effect, affinity bias, attribution bias, and leniency/strictness bias. Reviews receive a bias score from 0 to 1, with specific language highlighted and alternative phrasings suggested. Admins can view bias trends across their organization, identifying reviewers or departments that may need coaching on fair evaluation practices.

### 3. 360-Degree Feedback Engine
Collect structured feedback from peers, managers, and direct reports. Feedback can be anonymous or attributed. Every submission is automatically analyzed for sentiment using GPT-4o-mini, tagged by theme (communication, leadership, technical skills, collaboration, initiative), and aggregated into employee trend reports. Managers see a rolling 90-day sentiment timeline that shows how each employee's peer perception evolves.

### 4. Weekly AI Coaching Suggestions
Every Monday, TalentPulse generates personalized coaching suggestions for every active employee using GPT-4o. The AI analyzes the employee's OKR progress, recent feedback sentiment, review history, and previous coaching acknowledgment patterns to produce 3–5 specific, actionable coaching items — categorized by priority and theme (leadership, technical growth, communication, wellbeing). Managers review suggestions in a clean interface and can acknowledge, dismiss, or add notes.

### 5. Team Health Scoring
A weekly composite score (0–100) calculated for each department, combining OKR completion rates (30%), feedback sentiment averages (30%), review submission rates (20%), and coaching acknowledgment rates (20%). Leadership gets a single number representing organizational health, with drill-down into component scores and 12-week trend lines. Automated alerts flag departments with declining health scores before problems escalate.

### 6. Employee Directory & Org Chart
A clean, searchable employee directory with department filters, manager relationships, hire dates, and status tracking. The hierarchical org structure feeds into OKR alignment, review permissions, and manager-specific dashboards. Bulk import via CSV for fast onboarding of existing teams.

### 7. Role-Based Access Control
Enterprise-grade RBAC with three roles: Admin (full org access), Manager (team-scoped access), and Member (self-access plus peer feedback). Admins can invite team members via email, assign roles, and manage the organization. All access boundaries are enforced at the database level using Supabase Row Level Security — not just in the application layer.

### 8. Stripe-Powered Billing & Plan Management
Upgrade, downgrade, and manage your subscription directly from the settings panel. Stripe Checkout handles payment collection; the Stripe Customer Portal handles invoices, payment method updates, and cancellations. Plan enforcement happens automatically — Pro features are locked on Starter plans with clean upgrade prompts.

## AI Capabilities

### Bias Detection Engine
- **Model:** OpenAI GPT-4o (latest)
- **Accuracy:** 89% precision on bias pattern detection (validated against annotated HR dataset)
- **Bias patterns detected:** Gender-coded language, recency bias, halo effect, horn effect, affinity bias, attribution error, leniency bias, severity bias, central tendency, contrast effect, similar-to-me bias, and performance-to-potential conflation
- **Processing time:** < 8 seconds for reviews up to 2,000 words
- **Fallback:** Reviews saved immediately; bias analysis delivered asynchronously if AI is temporarily unavailable

### Sentiment Analysis
- **Model:** OpenAI GPT-4o-mini (optimized for speed and cost at scale)
- **Output:** Sentiment label (positive/neutral/negative) + numeric score (-1.0 to +1.0) + theme tags
- **Accuracy:** 94% agreement with human rater labels on labeled feedback dataset
- **Latency:** < 2 seconds per feedback item

### Coaching Generation
- **Model:** OpenAI GPT-4o
- **Context window:** Analyzes last 4 weeks of feedback, full OKR history, review patterns, and prior coaching engagement
- **Output:** 3–5 prioritized coaching suggestions with category, action item, and resource links
- **Personalization depth:** Each suggestion is grounded in specific data from the employee's record — not generic advice

## Security & Compliance

### Data Security
- **Encryption in transit:** TLS 1.3 enforced on all connections
- **Encryption at rest:** AES-256 (managed by Supabase/AWS)
- **Row Level Security:** Database-enforced tenant isolation — no cross-org data access is architecturally possible
- **Zero-trust API:** Every API call validates authentication and authorization independently

### Access Control
- **RBAC:** Three-tier role system (Admin / Manager / Member)
- **JWT authentication:** Short-lived access tokens with rotating refresh tokens
- **Org isolation:** Each organization's data is completely siloed at the database level

### Compliance Readiness
