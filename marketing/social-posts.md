### Post 3 — Feature Highlight: AI Bias Detection

I want to talk about something uncomfortable in performance management: bias.

Not malicious bias. Unconscious bias. The kind that shows up in the language we use without realizing it.

Research from MIT and Harvard shows that performance reviews contain measurable bias in **68% of cases**.

Some examples:
→ "He's a natural leader" vs. "She can be aggressive"
→ "He's confident" vs. "She's difficult to work with"
→ Focusing exclusively on recent events (recency bias) rather than the full year
→ Rating personality traits instead of actual performance outcomes

This isn't about blame. It's about data — and fixing a systemic problem that costs companies talent, culture, and real legal risk.

That's why we built AI bias detection into the core of TalentPulse.

Every review submitted through our platform is analyzed by GPT-4o for 12 bias patterns. Reviewers see:
• A bias score (0.0 = clean, 1.0 = highly biased)
• Highlighted passages flagged for review
• Alternative phrasings suggested in real time

Admins can see bias trends across reviewers and departments — so they can identify and address systemic patterns before they compound.

This isn't about policing language. It's about helping managers write better, fairer, more useful reviews.

Because when reviews are fair, employees trust them. And when employees trust feedback, they actually use it to grow.

### Post 5 — Product Tip: Weekly Coaching Workflow

Here's a workflow that top-performing managers at our customers use every Monday:

**The 10-Minute Monday Coaching Ritual**

1. Open TalentPulse (2 min)
   → Review the AI-generated coaching suggestions for each direct report
   → Each suggestion is grounded in their OKR progress, recent feedback, and review history

2. Triage by priority (3 min)
   → High priority: Bring into your next 1-on-1 directly
   → Medium priority: Note for the team meeting
   → Low priority: Acknowledge and monitor

3. Add a personal note (3 min)
   → You know context the AI doesn't. Add it.
   → "Jordan mentioned stress about the re-org — lean into the wellbeing suggestion this week"

4. Acknowledge and go (2 min)
   → Mark suggestions reviewed
   → TalentPulse logs your engagement for future coaching calibration

Total time: 10 minutes. Outcome: Every 1-on-1 this week has a specific, data-grounded development agenda.
